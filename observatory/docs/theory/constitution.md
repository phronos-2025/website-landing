# The P.H.R.O.N.O.S. Constitution

**Adopted: December 2024** | **Version 1.0**

## Preamble

We, the founding members of P.H.R.O.N.O.S. (Public Heuristics for Resilient Ontology, Navigation, and Observational Science), establish this constitution to guide our research, maintain intellectual integrity, and serve the public interest in understanding strategic foresight.

## Core Principles

### 1. Open Science

All research outputs shall be:
- Publicly accessible without paywalls
- Published under Creative Commons licenses
- Accompanied by reproducible methodologies
- Subject to open peer review

### 2. Epistemic Humility

We commit to:
- Acknowledging the limits of our knowledge
- Publishing uncertainties alongside findings
- Welcoming contradictory evidence
- Updating our frameworks when warranted

### 3. Methodological Transparency

We will:
- Document all analytical choices
- Publish negative results
- Share datasets and code
- Explain limitations explicitly

### 4. Living Documentation

Our work evolves through:
- Version-controlled updates
- Public changelog maintenance
- Archived historical versions
- Clear version identifiers

## Research Ethics

1. We prioritize truth over convenience
2. We credit all contributors appropriately
3. We engage critics in good faith
4. We correct errors promptly and visibly

## Governance

P.H.R.O.N.O.S. operates as:
- A non-profit research initiative
- A community-driven knowledge commons
- An open-review scholarly platform

---

**This constitution is itself a living document, subject to amendment through community consensus.**
